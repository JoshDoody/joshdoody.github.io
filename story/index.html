1: Avoid The Dreaded Salary Question

The first way to get a raise is unusual because you'll take advantage of it by not doing something that could cost you a lot of money.

I call this The Dreaded Salary Question, and it usually comes up early in the interview process, sometimes before you even start interviewing. It looks like this:

So where are you right now in terms of compensation, and what are you hoping for if you make this move?

Recruiter or Hiring Manager, early in the interivew process

Sound familiar? I bet it does.

Don't disclose your current or desired salary in a negotation and you'll make a lot more money throughout your career.

John didn't disclose his current or desired salaries, and look how it worked out for him!

2: Negotiate

If you do well in your job interviews, you'll get a job offer. The next way to get a raise is to negotiate when you receive a job offer. If you negotiate, you'll make a lot more money over the course of your career. If you just accept the job offer, you'll leave a lot of money on the table over the course of your career.

Justin almost passed on this opportunity, but he didn't! Instead, he negotiated his job offer and made $11,000 more last year with a single email! $6,000 of that was base salary, so he'll get more money every year. In five years, he'll have another $30,000 in his pocket just because he counteroffered.

{% include testimonials/justin_abrahms.html %}

When you get a job offer, you should counter somewhere between 10% and 20% above the offer. If you do this, you will make a lot more money throughout your career.

Shannon also negotiated her job offer and look how well she did!

3: The Final Discussion

Even if you counteroffer, you're not done negotiating—there's still another opportunity to make more money before you finalize your compensation package!

They'll usually come back to you with some sort of compromise—something less than your counter and more than their initial offer—to talk it over and finalize all the details. I call this The Final Discussion.

This is a chance for you to keep negotiating for more base salary and other benefits like extra vacation, a signing bonus, equity, or even a different work schedule.

Josh C. did a great job negotiating a big salary increase, but he didn't stop there. He also got an extra week of paid vacation each year. This is the best kind of raise because he'll get paid for entire week without even working!

{% include testimonials/josh_c.html %}

What about when you're staying put?

There are two great opportunities to make more money at your current job.

4: Getting promoted

Most people sit back and wait for promotions to come to them.

But you should actively pursue your next promotion, then your next one, and your next one so that you're always moving forward. You'll get more promotions and make more money that way.

Promotions are important because they usually move you into a different pay range, and that means you have more room for a bigger salary, and it usually means a pay bump just for moving into that new pay range!

5: Getting a raise

And we can't forget about good old fashioned raises!

Just like promotions, most people sit back and wait for merit increases or market adjustments to be granted by HR istead of going after them.

You should actively pursue raises so that you're always paid what you're worth and recognized for all the value you add to your company. Occasionally do some market research so you know your market value, and keep track of all the new skills and experience you contribute to your company as you add more and more value. When your value exceeds your salary, it's time to request a raise.

Summary: 5 ways to get a raise and supercharge your lifetime earnings

But enough about those other people. This is all about your story! Let's review the five ways to get a raise and supercharge your lifetime earnings:

  • Don't disclose your current or desired salary during your job interviews because that could cost you a lot of money.
  • Negotiate your job offers so you can make a lot more money every time you change jobs.
  • Kep negotiating even after you counter so you can maximize your starting salary and maximize your benefits.
  • Actively pursue promotions so you'll progress through your career more quickly.
  • Actively pursue raises so you're always paid what you're worth.

Do those five things and you'll make a lot more money throughout your career for doing the work you would've done anyway.
