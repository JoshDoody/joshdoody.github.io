Opportunity 1: The Dreaded Salary Question

Your first opportunity to make a lot ore money is unusual because you'll take advantage of it by not doing something that could cost you a lot of money.

I call it The Dreaded Salary Question, and it usually comes up early in the interview process, sometimes before you even start interviewing. It looks like this:

So where are you right now in terms of compensation, and what are you hoping for if you make this move?

Recruiter or Hiring Manager, early in the interivew process

Sound familiar? I bet it does.

You shouldn't disclose your current or desired salary in a negotation—it will cost you money if you do. Don't squander this opportunity by disclosing your current or desired salary.

John didn't disclose his current or desired salaries, and look how it worked out for him...

Opportunity 2: Counteroffer

If you do well in your interviews, you'll get a job offer. Your next opportunity to make a lot more money is how you respond to that job offer: if you negotiate, you'll make a lot more money over the course of your career; if you just accept the job offer, you'll cost yourself a lot of money.

Justin almost passed on this opportunity, but he didn't! Instead, he took advantage of this opportunity and made $11,000 more last year.

{% include testimonials/justin_abrahms.html %}

So when you get a job offer, you should counter somehwere between 10% and 20% above that offer. If you do this, you will make a lot more money throughout your career.

Shannon also countered and look how well she did!

{% include testimonials/shannon_long.html %}

Opportunity 3: The Final Discussion

Even if you counteroffer, you're not done negotiating! There's still another opportunity to make a bit more money before you finalize things!

They'll usually come back to you with some sort of compromise—something less than your counter and more than their initial offer. I call this The Final Discussion.

This is a chance for you to keep negotiating for a little more base salary and other benefits like extra vacation, a signing bonus, or even work from home a couple days a week.

Josh C. did a great job negotiating a big salary increase, but he didn't stop there! He also got an extra week of vacation each year...

{% include testimonials/josh_c.html %}

What about when you're staying put?

There are two great opportunities to make more money at your current job. One pays off later, and the other pays off immediately.

Opportunity 4: Getting promoted

Most people sit back and wait for promotions to come to them. But you won't do that because you want to progress as quickly as possible so you have more experience and a greater opportunity to get paid.

You'll actively pursue your next promotion, then your next one, and your next one so that you're always moving forward. You'll get more promotions and make more money that way.

Opportunity 5: Getting a raise

Just like promotions, most people sit back and wait for raises to come to them. They wait for merit increases or market adjustments to be granted by HR istead of just going after them.

You'll actively pursue raises so that you're always paid what you're worth and recognized for all the value you add to your company. All you need to do is occasionally do some market research so you know your market value, and keep track of all the new skills and experience you contribute to your company as you add more and more value.

But enough about those other people! This is all about your story! Let's review the five opportunities you will take advantage of to make a lot more money while you write the story of your career:

  • You'll actively pursue promotions so you'll progress through your career more quickly.

Do those five things and you'll make a lot more money throughout your career for doing the work you would've done anyway.
